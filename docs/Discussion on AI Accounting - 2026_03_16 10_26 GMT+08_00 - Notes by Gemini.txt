Summary
Initial discussion explored destructive AI accounting software development, focusing on internal productivity with plans for eventual external sale or acquisition.

Rethinking AI Accounting Systems
The group decided against building a conventional accounting system, preferring to create "destructive" AI software to eliminate human key-in of sales, purchases, and bank statements. The goal is to reimagine accounting by having AI process files, generate reports, and eventually reduce human verification to 10%. The major decision was to focus on a solution that minimizes human manpower via an AI agent that captures data and instantly generates reports for 500 clients with minimal staff.

Technical Viability and Ecosystem
Technical analysis confirmed the viability of using OCR and AI to extract text from scanned documents and format it into useful data for reporting via API to systems like Zeno or Quickbook. The proposed system integrates CRM, payments, and accounting, creating an AI ecosystem that processes categorized documents and sets automated payment schedules.

Focusing on Internal Productivity
The team shifted strategy from a client-facing subscription model to a two-phase approach, with Phase 1 focusing on internal productivity to drastically cut down on staff needed to manage assignments. This internal case study is intended to prove effectiveness and attract potential acquisition interest from larger firms, avoiding direct competition with existing accounting software companies.


Details
* Initial Discussion on AI and Accounting Software: Crystalbelle Lee initiated the conversation by referring to a prepared interface and seeking further input on its design. George Yeo inquired about Crystalbelle Lee’s background in HR and then brought up "open cloud" as a current hot topic in the market, noting its potential to consolidate data without human key-in (00:00:00). The discussion then shifted to creating "destructive" accounting software, similar to JRO, that could significantly reduce the need for human manpower in tasks like processing sales and purchase invoices, bank statements, and expenses (00:01:43).
* Rethinking Accounting in the AI Era: George Yeo clarified they were not looking to build a conventional accounting system, but rather rethink how accounting should be done in the AI era. Boon Seong Low suggested a complete re-imagining where users could throw files into a folder, and AI would process them, generate reports, with minimal human verification needed, moving from possibly 50% initial human verification to eventually 10% (00:03:01).
* Platform Integration and Data Processing Flow: Crystalbelle Lee proposed the idea of a platform visible to both customers and accountants, potentially integrating with existing accounting software for necessary reporting (00:04:29). George Yeo identified two main portions of the accounting process: collecting data and generating the accounting report. The current system involves clients preparing and passing data to staff who key it in, and the goal is to shift from needing five people to manage 50 clients to one person and one AI system managing 50 assignments (00:05:22).
* AI Agent for Data Entry and Reporting: George Yeo proposed that the AI agent could capture data anywhere, minimizing the need for human data entry staff, and immediately generate reports. The aim is to handle 500 clients with only one to five staff members and one AI system, compared to the current average of one staff member per 10 clients (00:06:49). Crystalbelle Lee asked if a standardized template could facilitate data entry (00:08:13).
* Minimizing Human Key-in and Bank Reconciliation: George Yeo discussed how government regulations moving towards e-invoicing will simplify data capture for accounting systems (00:08:13). George Yeo noted that current accounting systems like JRO already perform bank statement reconciliation and that the goal is to minimize the portion requiring human key-in. Crystalbelle Lee confirmed that auto-recording linked to bank accounts is possible (00:09:39).
* Agentic AI Solution Over Traditional Software: Boon Seong Low suggested that the key breakthrough would be focusing on the processing part rather than using conventional accounting software. They envisioned a "CPU" that processes all the data and generates reports, regardless of the client's source software (00:10:47). George Yeo agreed that building an accounting system would mean competing in a crowded market, preferring a tool that others would use (00:21:59).
* Technical Viability of Data Extraction and Processing: Eng Keen Tan explained the technical viability of the proposed system, suggesting that once documents are scanned into a folder, OCR (Optical Character Recognition) can extract the text. The extracted text is then passed to AI, which formats it into useful data (invoice numbers, amounts, etc.), which is then used to generate reports that can be sent to systems like Zeno or Quickbook via API (00:14:00).
* Integrating AI for Comprehensive Administrative Support: George Yeo outlined a system where customers place categorized documents in folders, and the AI agent processes the data and sets payment schedules. The system could prompt clients about due payments and potentially auto-make payments, creating an "ecosystem" that integrates CRM, payment methods, accounting, and administrative work for the client (00:17:39).
* Business Model and Competition Concerns: George Yeo proposed an end-user-facing subscription model where clients pay a small fee (e.g., $50) for the AI system, rather than paying an accountant $500, arguing this positions the AI as a complement to existing accounting software (00:19:16). Eng Keen Tan expressed concern that this client-facing subscription model would eventually compete with and be copied by accounting software companies (00:25:23). Boon Seong Low agreed that the accounting software companies already have AI teams and might copy the solution or not want to partner at all (00:28:28).
* Focusing on Internal Productivity as Phase One: Boon Seong Low suggested a two-phase approach, with Phase One focusing on using George Yeo's company as a case study to significantly cut down on the number of people needed to process assignments. This internal focus would explore integration options and change client handling processes, serving as a project for joint development (00:32:56). The immediate goal is to reduce five people managing 50 assignments down to two people, and then scale up to manage 100 or 200 assignments with two people (00:30:45).
* Strategic Direction and Market Entry: The consensus shifted towards first focusing on internal productivity to create a solution that proves effectiveness and then potentially selling or being acquired by a bigger firm, rather than aiming for a global product launch and competition with VC-backed startups (00:33:51). Boon Seong Low emphasized that once the process is fixed, the system's increment will be exponential, not linear (00:35:49). George Yeo agreed that this strategy would attract potential acquisition interest from larger firms that would rather buy than build a competitive system (00:36:50).
* Exploring Business-to-Business (B2B) and Freelancer Models: They discussed potential business models beyond B2C, including B2B solutions or wholesaling the tool to freelance accountants who may have lost their jobs, enabling them to become entrepreneurs (00:38:29). The immediate next step is to build the agentic AI and go to market to explore who the target customers are (00:39:03).
* Next Steps and Commercials: They agreed to meet on Friday to discuss the commercials and aim to use government grants to cover the investment in the system (00:39:51) (00:41:40). Boon Seong Low noted that the accounting industry is safer from outside disruptors due to regulatory compliance, giving them more time to work (00:42:29).
* Technical and Cost Considerations for AI Engine: Eng Keen Tan raised the high cost of calling external AI services and suggested they should eventually host their own open-source Large Language Model (LLM) and customize it to be accounting-based (00:43:27). This would provide a proprietary technical edge and increase the company's valuation (00:44:25). The cost of using external AI is consumption-based (per call) for enterprises and could reach $20,000 per month, essentially replacing staff costs (00:45:16).
